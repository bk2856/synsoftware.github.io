e-Business Development

 

Once the e-business strategy is agreed upon and the platform is selected, the development effort can start. Typical development process would have several steps

functional design
technical design
Use Cases and UML models, process flows
graphics design specifications
coding of requests, responses, processes, subprocesses
unit and system testing
implementation scripts
training and support and so on. 

Unlike traditional legacy or client server development, the methodologies found effective in e-business system development are tailored to the special needs of Internet transaction based systems.

Some of the unique requirements in e-business development are:

How is a strategy document converted to site-map?
How is traditional use case converted to a request-response model?
How does GUI specs incorporate both graphics and data specification?
How is the technical specification translated to UML or similar models?
How is the models translated to coder level specifications?
How to employ form/request/data level security along with function level authorities?
How to support print, batch process, EDI functions without compromising MVC structure?
How to integrate Document servers, mail servers and so on?
How to build customer support function right into the processes?
How to be compliant with other servers or exchanges?
How to prove scalability?
How to prove production?

A typical web development company who designs fancy storefronts cannot accommodate these requirements, because these requirements don't fall into their level of expertise.

A typical system development company also cannot do a good job because, these are new to them. 

Most popular answer to dilemma is to enroll the services of a big-name outfit who is neither of the above hoping that they will do a good job. This results in bloated budgets and botched deadlines .
